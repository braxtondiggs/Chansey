# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

Beginners exploring cryptocurrency trading. These are people new to crypto who need guidance, simplicity, and
reassurance. They're looking for a low-friction way to start investing in crypto without feeling overwhelmed by charts,
jargon, or complex trading interfaces. The interface should make them feel safe, informed, and in control of their
financial journey.

### Brand Personality

**Modern, approachable, empowering.** Cymbit Trading (internally "Chansey") sits in the Acorns-for-crypto space — making
cryptocurrency investing feel as natural and low-stress as saving spare change. The tone is friendly and encouraging
without being patronizing. It respects the user's intelligence while removing unnecessary complexity.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Visual tone**: Clean, warm, approachable — inspired by Acorns and Coinbase/Robinhood's consumer-friendly aesthetic.
  Generous whitespace, rounded forms, clear hierarchy. Premium but not cold.
- **References**: Acorns (primary — simplicity, guided investing, friendly onboarding), Coinbase (clean crypto UI),
  Robinhood (accessible finance)
- **Anti-references**: NOT Binance/KuCoin (cluttered, overwhelming). NOT generic corporate SaaS (soulless dashboards).
  NOT intimidating — nothing that makes crypto feel complex or scary to beginners.
- **Theme**: User-configurable. Three PrimeNG preset themes (Aura, Lara, Nora) with full control over primary color and
  surface color. No enforced default color — design must work well across all color combinations and both light/dark
  modes.
- **Typography**: InterDisplay (display/headings), Lato (body/UI text). Full weight range available (100-900).
- **Component library**: PrimeNG with TailwindCSS utilities. Design tokens via `--p-*` CSS custom properties.

### Design Principles

1. **Simplicity over density** — Every screen should feel breathable. Prefer progressive disclosure over showing
   everything at once. If a beginner would feel overwhelmed, simplify.

2. **Guide, don't gate** — Use clear labels, helpful tooltips, and contextual education to help users understand crypto
   concepts. The UI should teach as it goes without blocking progress.

3. **Theme-resilient design** — All designs must look excellent across Aura/Lara/Nora presets, any primary color, any
   surface color, and both light and dark modes. Never hard-code colors; always use design tokens.

4. **Confidence through clarity** — Financial interfaces need trust. Use consistent patterns, predictable interactions,
   and clear feedback. Show users what's happening with their money at every step.

5. **Mobile-first, PWA-ready** — Designed for the phone-first user. Touch targets, responsive layouts, and
   offline-capable patterns are non-negotiable.

### Technical Design Constraints

- Angular 20 standalone components
- PrimeNG component library (Aura/Lara/Nora presets)
- TailwindCSS for utility styling
- PrimeNG design tokens (`--p-*`) for all color/spacing references
- Dark mode via `.app-dark` class on root element
- InterDisplay font for headings, Lato for body
- PWA with service worker support
